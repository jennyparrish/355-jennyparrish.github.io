Washington State's Pacific Rhododendron

"Rhodies"

Rhododendron macrophyllum

Also called Coast Rhododendrons and California Rhododendrons

Washington's beloved "Rose tree with big leaves" is one of only five native rhododendron species in the state.

Can be found wildly growing west of the Cascades crest, from the western coast of central California all the way up to northern British Columbia.

Often found around 1-5 meters in height (3-15ft), they have been known to grow as high as 30 feet tall. Being forced to stretch higher to reach sunlight that is so often blocked out by the towering neighboring evergreen perennials, they actually grow taller in the shade. They grow well among the lowland rain-forest conifers of the Pacific Northwest, prefering damp soil or dry.

Is a cousin of azaleas.

Are a favorite food of bumblebees, bees, flies, beetles, moths, and hummingbirds.

People have gotten sick eating honey from bees who source their pollen from Rhodies - came to be known as "mad honey" due to the andromedotoxin that is produced.
